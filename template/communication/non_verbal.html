Non-Verbal Communication

Understand the power of body language

Overview

Non-verbal communication refers to the transmission of messages without using words. It includes body language, facial expressions, gestures, posture, and eye contact. In professional settings, non-verbal cues often communicate more than spoken words.

Major Components

  • Facial expressions
  • Eye contact
  • Gestures
  • Posture and body movement
  • Personal appearance
  • Proxemics (personal space)

Importance in Interviews

  • Creates first impression
  • Shows confidence level
  • Reflects professionalism
  • Builds trust with interviewer
  • Supports verbal communication

Tips to Improve Non-Verbal Skills

  • Maintain proper eye contact
  • Keep an upright posture
  • Use natural hand gestures
  • Control nervous movements
  • Maintain a pleasant facial expression
  • Dress professionally

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Avoiding eye contact
  • Slouching posture
  • Excessive hand movement
  • Nervous habits (pen clicking, fidgeting)
  • Unprofessional appearance
