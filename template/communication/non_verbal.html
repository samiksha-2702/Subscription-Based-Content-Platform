Non-Verbal Communication

Speak without words · Master body language · Build instant trust

Overview

Non-verbal communication refers to transmitting messages without words — body language, facial expressions, gestures, posture, and eye contact. Research shows that 55% of communication is body language, 38% is tone of voice, and only 7% are actual words (Mehrabian's 7-38-55 rule).

Key insight: Your body often reveals what your words hide. Mastering non-verbal cues makes you more persuasive, confident, and trustworthy.

Positive vs Negative Signals

Positive Signals

  • ✓ Steady eye contact (60-70% of time)
  • ✓ Upright, open posture
  • ✓ Firm handshake (2-3 pumps)
  • ✓ Genuine smile, slight head nods
  • ✓ Palms visible → honesty

Negative Signals

  • ✗ Avoiding eye contact or staring
  • ✗ Slouching, crossed arms, fidgeting
  • ✗ Weak or crushing handshake
  • ✗ Checking phone/watch, tapping feet

Tips to Improve

  • Maintain natural eye contact (look at nose bridge if nervous)
  • Keep upright, open posture — never slouch
  • Use deliberate hand gestures to emphasize points
  • Control nervous habits (pen clicking, hair touching)
  • Keep a pleasant, approachable facial expression
  • Dress appropriately for the company culture
  • Power pose for 2 minutes before high-stakes events
  • Record yourself on video to spot habits

Practice exercise: Record a 2-min mock interview. Watch without sound — your body language tells everything.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Avoiding eye contact or staring too intensely
  • Slouching or leaning too far back
  • Excessive hand movements or fidgeting
  • Nervous habits (pen clicking, hair twirling, foot tapping)
  • Unprofessional or wrinkled appearance
  • Crossing arms (defensive signal)
  • Forced or fake smiling
  • Invading personal space

Cultural Differences & Personal Space

Personal Space Zones

  • Intimate (0-18″) – close family
  • Personal (1.5-4′) – friends
  • Social (4-12′) – professional settings
  • Public (12′+) – presentations

Cultural Variations

  • Eye contact: Direct in West, indirect in East Asia
  • Gestures: "OK" sign varies globally
  • Space: Smaller in Latin America, larger in Northern Europe
  • Smiling: Meaning differs by culture

Virtual Non-Verbals

  • Look at camera (not screen) for eye contact
  • Good lighting on face, upright seated posture
  • Nod visibly to show active listening
  • Minimize background distractions

7-Day Practice Routine

  • Day 1-2: Mirror practice — observe your expressions

  • Day 5-6: Practice handshake & entry/exit posture
  • Day 7: Real-world focus on eye contact all day
