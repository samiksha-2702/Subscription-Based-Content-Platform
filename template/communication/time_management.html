Time Management

Introduction

Time management is the systematic process of planning and organizing how time is allocated to specific activities. Effective time management enables students and professionals to increase productivity, reduce stress, and achieve goals efficiently.

Objectives of Time Management

  • Improve productivity and efficiency
  • Meet deadlines consistently
  • Reduce academic and workplace stress
  • Maintain work-life balance
  • Achieve short-term and long-term goals

Key Techniques

Prioritization (Eisenhower Matrix)

  • Urgent and Important – Do immediately
  • Important but Not Urgent – Schedule
  • Urgent but Not Important – Delegate
  • Neither Urgent nor Important – Eliminate

Pomodoro Technique

  • Work for 25 minutes
  • Take a 5-minute break
  • After four cycles, take a longer break

Time Blocking

Time blocking involves assigning fixed time slots to tasks in advance. This reduces multitasking and improves focus.

Common Time Wasters

  • Procrastination
  • Excessive social media usage
  • Poor planning
  • Unnecessary meetings
  • Frequent interruptions
💡 Plan your next day before sleeping to improve morning productivity.

Benefits for Students

  • Better exam preparation
  • Timely assignment submission
  • Reduced anxiety
  • Improved academic performance
