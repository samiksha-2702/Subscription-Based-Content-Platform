Interview & Selection Process

Typical Rounds

  • Online coding challenge (2-4 problems)
  • Phone screen (technical + behavioral)
  • On-site/Virtual loop (4-5 interviews)
  • Coding + DSA interviews (2-3 rounds)
  • System Design (for experienced roles)
  • Googleyness & Leadership interview

Key Focus Areas

  • Data Structures & Algorithms
  • System Design (scalability)
  • Problem-solving approach & optimization
  • Clean, readable code
  • Cultural fit (Googleyness)
  • Communication & collaboration
Prep Tip: Practice LeetCode Medium/Hard problems. Focus on graph algorithms, DP, and system design. Review Google interview experiences on Blind and Reddit.

Google Culture ("Googleyness")

  • Open and transparent culture
  • Psychological safety & inclusion
  • 20% time for personal projects
  • Data-driven decision making
  • Fail fast, learn faster mindset
  • Collaboration over hierarchy
  • Focus on user experience
Glassdoor: 4.5/5 Best Places to Work

Salary & Benefits

  • L3 (New Grad): $180k - $200k total comp
  • L4 (SDE II): $250k - $300k total comp
  • L5 (Senior): $350k - $450k total comp
  • Base salary + bonus + RSUs (stocks)
  • Free meals & snacks (on-site)
  • On-site gym, wellness programs
  • Tuition reimbursement & learning budget
  • Parental leave (up to 24 weeks)
  • Health, dental, vision insurance

Employee Insights

  • Innovative and flexible work culture
  • Strong focus on research and development (X, DeepMind)
  • Glassdoor rating: ~4.5 / 5
  • Benefits include wellness programs and learning support
  • Hybrid work model (3 days in office)
  • High internal mobility across teams
  • Global relocation support
Insider Tip: Google values "intellectual humility." It's okay to say "I don't know" – show how you'd find the answer.
