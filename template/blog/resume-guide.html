Create a professional resume that stands out to recruiters

Introduction

A well-crafted resume is your first step toward landing your dream job. It's your personal marketing document that tells recruiters who you are, what you've accomplished, and why they should hire you. In today's competitive job market, a strong resume can be the difference between getting an interview or being overlooked.

Recruiters spend 6-7 seconds on average 75% of resumes rejected by ATS

Resume Structure

  • Header: Full name, phone, email, LinkedIn/GitHub links
  • Professional Summary: 2-3 lines summarizing your key strengths
  • Education: Degrees, universities, GPA (if 3.5+), relevant coursework
  • Skills: Technical skills, programming languages, tools, soft skills
  • Experience: Jobs, internships with bullet points and metrics
  • Projects: Personal, academic, or open-source projects
  • Certifications & Awards: Relevant certificates and achievements
Pro Tip: For students, keep resume to 1 page. For experienced professionals, 2 pages maximum.

Powerful Action Verbs

Start each bullet point with a strong action verb to make your achievements stand out.

Developed Designed Implemented Led Managed Created Optimized Improved Increased Reduced Launched Built Automated Collaborated Achieved Delivered

Resume Writing Tips

  • Keep resume to 1 page for students and new grads
  • Use strong action verbs to start each bullet point
  • Quantify achievements with numbers and metrics
  • Use clean, consistent formatting with adequate white space
  • Tailor resume to each job description
  • Include ATS-friendly keywords from job posting
  • Save as PDF to preserve formatting
  • Proofread twice - typos get resumes rejected
Quantification Examples:

❌ "Improved app performance"
✅ "Reduced load time by 40% affecting 100K+ users"

Before vs After: Resume Bullet Points

Weak/Bad Examples

"Responsible for writing code"
"Worked on a team project"
"Helped fix bugs"

Strong/Good Examples

"Developed 5+ microservices handling 10K requests/sec"
"Led a team of 4 to deliver project 2 weeks ahead of schedule"
"Identified and resolved 50+ critical bugs, reducing downtime by 30%"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Long paragraphs instead of bullet points
  • Including irrelevant skills or outdated technologies
  • Generic descriptions without specific achievements
  • Poor formatting or inconsistent font usage
  • Spelling and grammar errors
  • Using first-person pronouns (I, me, my)
  • Including a photo or personal details (age, marital status)
  • Lying or exaggerating experience

ATS Optimization Tips

  • Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
  • Include keywords from the job description
  • Avoid tables, columns, graphics, and images
  • Use a simple, clean font (Arial, Calibri, Inter)
  • Save as .docx or .pdf (check company preference)
  • Spell out acronyms (e.g., JavaScript instead of JS)
  • Submit as a single-column layout
ATS Fact: Over 75% of resumes are rejected by Applicant Tracking Systems before a human ever sees them. Optimize yours!

Resume Pre-Submission Checklist

Resume length appropriate (1-2 pages)
Contact info correct and up to date
Action verbs start each bullet point
Achievements quantified with numbers
No spelling or grammar errors
Tailored to specific job description
ATS-friendly format and keywords
Saved as PDF (unless Word requested)
