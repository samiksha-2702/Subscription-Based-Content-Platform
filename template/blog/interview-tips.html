Introduction

Cracking interviews at top tech companies requires strong technical skills, problem-solving ability, and effective communication. This guide compiles the best tips from successful candidates and industry experts to help you ace your next interview.

50+ tips curated Used by 10,000+ candidates

Coding Interview Tips

  • Practice problems on LeetCode, HackerRank, and Codeforces
  • Focus on data structures: arrays, linked lists, trees, graphs, hash tables
  • Master algorithms: recursion, DP, BFS/DFS, greedy, divide & conquer
  • Clarify the problem before coding and consider edge cases
  • Write clean, readable code with meaningful variable names
  • Explain your thought process out loud during interviews
  • Analyze time & space complexity before coding
  • Practice with a timer to simulate real conditions
Pro Tip: Aim to solve LeetCode Medium problems in under 20 minutes. Quality > Quantity - understand patterns, not memorize solutions.

System Design Tips

  • Learn scalability: horizontal vs vertical scaling
  • Understand load balancing, caching (Redis, CDN)
  • Database concepts: SQL vs NoSQL, sharding, replication
  • Practice designing: URL shortener, chat app, social media feed
  • Use diagrams to explain architecture clearly
  • Discuss trade-offs (performance vs consistency vs cost)
  • Start simple, then add complexity
  • Know CAP theorem and when to apply
Common System Design Questions:

Design Twitter/X, Uber, YouTube, Dropbox, Web Crawler, Rate Limiter

Behavioral Interview Tips

  • Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
  • Prepare 5-7 strong stories covering different scenarios
  • Practice common questions: "Tell me about yourself", "Why this company?"
  • Show teamwork, leadership, and conflict resolution examples
  • Maintain confident body language and clear communication
  • Be honest - don't exaggerate accomplishments
  • Research company values and align your answers
  • Prepare questions to ask the interviewer
Tell me about yourself (2-min pitch)
Why do you want to work here?
Describe a challenge you overcame
How do you handle conflict?
Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

STAR Method Deep Dive

  • S - Situation: Set the context (1-2 sentences)
  • T - Task: What was your responsibility?
  • A - Action: What specific steps did you take?
  • R - Result: What was the outcome? Include metrics!
Example STAR Answer:

"S: Our team missed a deadline... T: I needed to fix the bug... A: I debugged, collaborated with X, implemented fix... R: Delivered 2 days early, saved $10K."

Mock Interviews & Practice Resources

  • Take mock interviews with peers or mentors weekly
  • Use interviewing.io for anonymous practice
  • Review experiences on Glassdoor and Blind
  • Track progress and revisit weak areas systematically
  • Record yourself to analyze body language and speech
  • Join Discord/Telegram groups for peer feedback

Daily Preparation Plan

  • Morning (1-2 hours): Solve coding problems (2-3 LeetCode)
  • Afternoon (30 mins): Revise system design concepts
  • Evening (30 mins): Behavioral practice + STAR stories
  • Weekend (2 hours): Full mock interview + review mistakes
  • Daily (10 mins): Review yesterday's weak areas
Recommended Schedule (60 days before interview):

Month 1: Build fundamentals + easy problems
Month 2: Medium/Hard problems + mocks + system design

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Not practicing consistently (last-minute cramming)
  • ❌ Ignoring time complexity analysis
  • ❌ Rushing into coding without planning
  • ❌ Giving vague or unprepared HR answers
  • ❌ Not researching the company before interview
  • ❌ Failing to ask questions at the end
  • ❌ Poor communication during problem solving
  • ❌ Not following up with thank-you email
